Christina Tosi

Christina Tosi is a renowned pastry chef, author, and entrepreneur known for her innovative approach to desserts and her unique baking style. She is the founder of Milk Bar, a bakery and dessert chain with locations across the United States and in Canada. Tosi has won numerous awards and accolades throughout her career, including being named the James Beard Foundation's Rising Star Chef of the Year in 2012, and being recognized as one of Forbes' 30 Under 30 in the food and wine industry in 2014. She has authored multiple cookbooks, including the New York Times bestseller "Milk Bar Life," and has been a judge on the television shows MasterChef and MasterChef Junior. In addition to her accomplishments in the culinary world, Tosi is also an advocate for mental health awareness and has spoken publicly about her own struggles with anxiety and depression.

Why do I like baking?

People have many reasons for enjoying baking. Here are some common reasons:
  1. Creative expression: Baking allows people to express their creativity and experiment with different ingredients and techniques.
  2. Sense of accomplishment: Baking can be a challenging and rewarding task. When a recipe turns out well, it can give a sense of accomplishment and boost self-confidence.
  3. Relaxation: Baking can be a stress-reliever for some people. The repetitive motions of mixing and measuring ingredients can be calming and therapeutic.
  4. Sharing with others: Baking is often associated with sharing food with others. Making treats for friends, family, or colleagues can be a way to show appreciation and strengthen relationships.
  5. Nostalgia: Baking can evoke memories of childhood or special occasions. The aromas and tastes of certain baked goods can bring back fond memories

The Institute of Culinary Education (ICE)

The Institute of Culinary Education (ICE) is a culinary school located in New York City that offers a wide range of programs in the culinary arts, pastry and baking arts, and hospitality management. ICE is accredited by the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges and offers both diploma and degree programs.

Founded in 1975 by Peter Kump, ICE has a strong reputation for providing hands-on culinary education and training, with state-of-the-art facilities and experienced faculty members. In addition to traditional culinary courses, ICE also offers classes in food writing, food photography, wine and beverage studies, and more.

ICE has a strong focus on career development and offers job placement assistance to graduates, helping them to secure positions in top restaurants, hotels, and other food-related businesses. The school has a diverse student body and attracts students from all over the world who are passionate about food and cooking.
